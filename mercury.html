Mercury

Mercury is the closest planet to the sun and due to it proximately is not easly to seen except during twilight.

For every two orbits of the sun, mercury completes three rotation about its axis and up until 1965 it was thought that the same side of mercury constantly faced the sun. Thirteen times a century Mercury can be observed from the earth passing across the face of the sun in an event called transit

Mercury facts

  • Diameter: 4,879 km
  • Mass: 3.29 x10^23 kg (0,06 Earth)
  • Moons: None
  • Orbits Distance: 57,999,227 km (0.39 AU)
  • Orbit period: 88 days
  • Surface Temperature: -137° to 427°c
  • First Record: 14th century BC
  • Recovery By: Assyrian astronomers
